## üñ±Ô∏è How to Use (3 Simple Steps)

### **Step 1: Select What You Want to Modify**

**Option A: Select a Scene**
- Click on the **scene header** (the colored number or title area)
- The entire scene highlights with a purple border
- Hold `Cmd` (Mac) or `Ctrl` (Windows) to select multiple scenes

**Option B: Select a Frame**
- Click on any **frame image** (first or last frame)
- A "Selected" badge appears on the frame
- Hold `Cmd/Ctrl` to select multiple frames

**Option C: Select a Script**
- Hover over the script area
- Click the **pointer icon** that appears
- The script box highlights with purple border
- Hold `Cmd/Ctrl` to select multiple scripts

### **Step 2: Type Your Modification**

A bar appears at the bottom with your selection summary.

Type what you want to change in natural language:

**Examples:**
- "Make it more energetic and upbeat"
- "Shorten to 3 seconds"
- "Change the setting to a park"
- "Make her expression happier"
- "Add more emphasis on the benefits"

### **Step 3: Submit**

- Press `Enter` or click the send button
- AI processes your request
- Changes apply automatically
- Selection clears

Done! Your storyboard is updated.

## ‚ùì FAQ

**Q: Can I select scenes AND frames at the same time?**  
A: No, you can only select one type at a time (all scenes, or all frames, or all scripts). This keeps modifications focused.

**Q: What if I don't like the AI's changes?**  
A: Just make another selection and request different changes, or use Cmd+Z (undo is auto-saved in version history).

**Q: How specific should my modification request be?**  
A: Be as specific or general as you want:
- Specific: "Make scene 2 exactly 4.5 seconds long"
- General: "Make it more fun and energetic"

Both work! The AI interprets your intent.

**Q: Can I modify the timeline itself?**  
A: Yes! Click scenes in the timeline to select them, then request modifications.

**Q: Will modifications regenerate frames automatically?**  
A: Not automatically. After modifying frame prompts, you can regenerate specific frames by selecting them again and requesting regeneration, or proceed to full video generation.
